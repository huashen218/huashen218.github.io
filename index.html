With the goal of aligning AI and human values, my research strives to empower humans to interactively explain, evaluate, collaborate with AI, and further provide human feedback to enhance AI development. From a comprehensive view of Bidirectional Humans↔AI Alignment (systematic review of 400+ papers). From a human-centered perspective, my research enhances the explainability and usability of deployed AI systems through interaction, enabling humans to better understand and collaborate with AI (e.g., ConvXAI (CSCW'23 Best Demo), HypoCompass (AIED'24 Best Paper)). From an AI-centered perspective, my research aims to integrate human feedback into AI, by developing approaches to measure human values and incorporating them into AI development and customization (e.g., MultiTurnCleanup (EMNLP'23, Google Research Scholarship), Speaker Fairness (ICASSP'22, Amazon Alexa AI)).

My research is rooted in HCI and intersects with various AI fields, including NLP, speech, computer vision, and data science. I use mixed research methods, such as quantitative and qualitative human studies and AI algorithm & interaction design, to foster bidirectional alignment comprehensively.
